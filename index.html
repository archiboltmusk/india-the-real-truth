100 Volumes 10 Master Arcs Primary Sources Only No Heroes · No Villains Every Volume Ends in Action

This is not an encyclopedia. It is an architectural blueprint for a civilisational resurrection — approached with the precision of a surgeon and the soul of a philosopher.

The collection refuses the comfortable story taught in classrooms: that Partition was a clean, inevitable religious war between a unified Hindu crowd and a unified Muslim crowd, led by a cartoon villain and a flawless saint. It opens, instead, the doors to the air-conditioned boardrooms where an elite traded human lives for a monopoly on the state.

And it refuses to stay inside a single region's lens. The same boardroom deadlock is read again through the world's sharpest instruments for human absurdity, ego and crowd behaviour — Kafka's bureaucratic nightmares, Freud's theatre of the unconscious, Holmes's forensic deduction, and the pop-cultural machinery of mass intoxication — so that the dates stop being dates and start being a psychological thriller everyone can feel.

The Metric for Pure Truth

LAW 01

The Primary Source Rule

No reliance on post-1947 state-edited textbooks. Every claim rests on unedited letters, private diaries, court transcripts and legislative archives.

LAW 02

The No-Hero Constraint

No figure is a flawless saint or a cartoon villain. Their statesmanship and their catastrophic errors are laid bare, side by side.

LAW 03

The Actionable Pivot

Every volume closes with The Protocol — translating its historical truth into an immediate plan for the reader's own mind and community.

LAW 04

The Multidisciplinary Mirror

Every archival fact is re-read through a global psychological, literary or cultural lens — never to decorate the history, but to make its human mechanics impossible to look away from.
