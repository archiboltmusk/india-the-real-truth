The Protocol

PR-01

Detoxify the Mind

Recognise manufactured outrage and vote-bank panic for what they are. Read the deadlock, not the slogan.

PR-02

Go to the Source

Before repeating a historical claim, ask which letter, transcript or archive it rests on. If there is none, set it down.

PR-03

Build the Local Node

Turn your street into an independent node of the Samaj — a library, a mutual aid circle, a place to disagree without fracture.

PR-04

Dismantle the Pop-Hook

Treat every short, emotionally charged slogan as a hacked rhythm, not an argument. Ask what data point it is hiding while it makes you feel.

PR-05

Reject the Kafkaesque Label

Refuse the bureaucrat's box — majority, minority, vote-bank. Meet the person, not the category the state assigned them.

Editorial note: this work advances a specific, contested interpretation of subcontinental history — including claims about the Cabinet Mission Plan, the causes of Partition, and the colonial economic drain — that are debated by historians. It is presented here as the author's thesis, not as settled scholarly consensus. Readers are encouraged to consult the named primary sources directly and weigh competing interpretations.
